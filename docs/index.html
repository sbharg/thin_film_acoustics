Introduction

Motivated by the acoustowetting phenomenon and its applications to the dynamic wetting of objects by a coating liquid, we explore the influence of surface acoustic waves and gravity on the motion of a film flowing over a surface. We consider surfaces that may be both inclined and include topographical features like trenches, mounds, bumps, etc. Using the lubrication approximation, we reduce the equations of motion for the film to a single nonlinear partial differential equation that describes the evolution of the film height relative to the surface topography in time and space.
